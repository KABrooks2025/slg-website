The Problem

The real enemy is financial drift.

Most people were taught how to earn money. Almost no one was taught how to govern it. The result is financial drift — earning, spending, giving, and leading without assignment, ownership, or stewardship.

"Financial drift is not a money problem. It is a direction problem."
Book Kevin to Speak
Primary Enemy
Financial Drift
What happens when people earn without governing. Financial drift shows up as:
  • Earning money but never governing it
  • Spending without assignment or direction
  • Confusing income with ownership
  • Letting appetite direct your dollars
  • Avoiding your numbers out of shame
  • Treating fundraising like begging
  • Delaying asset-building until "later"
"No dollar without an assignment. No life without ownership."
The Foundation

The 15 Laws of Asset Identity

01
You are not broke because you lack income. You are broke because you lack ownership.
02
A consumer asks, "Can I afford this?" An owner asks, "Will this make me stronger?"
03
Money without identity will always return to old habits.
04
Financial literacy is not optional. It is adult survival.
05
The goal is not to look wealthy. The goal is to become structurally strong.
06
Income feeds the moment. Assets feed the future.
07
Stewardship means every dollar has an assignment.
08
If you do not direct your money, your appetites will.
09
A producer builds value before demanding reward.
10
Net worth is not just a number. It is evidence of decisions repeated over time.
11
Debt is not always evil, but ignorance with debt is dangerous.
12
Your gifts, knowledge, relationships, and discipline are all assets before money ever arrives.
13
Wealth starts within, but it must eventually show up in behavior, ownership, and structure.
14
Fundraising is not begging. Fundraising is transferring belief into resources.
15
The future belongs to people who know how to identify, develop, and deploy assets.
